The Why, What, and Who of Bmore Local

Why did we create Bmore Local?

Over a beer one night, we realized it was time to stop not hearing about all of the cool events that happen in Baltimore like trapeze artistry in Station North, food-making meet-ups, and taco crawls. Â Given how busy everyone is, we thought that it would be great to be able to quickly scan tweets related to interesting events.

What is Bmore Local?

The purpose of BmoreLocal is to make it easy to find interesting events. Â The twitter feeds are the result of crowd-sourcing. Â We have selected certain tweeters to be Event Mavens, which means every time they tweet about an event and use the relevant hash tag like #bmoart, their tweet shows up in the general and Art feed. Â We plan on starting with events and then looking at topic related feeds at some point soon. Â If you want to check out our code, just go to http://github.com/awgrover/curated-hash-feed .

How can you get involved with Bmore Local?

We are looking for curators, event mavens and people to spread the word about this project and give us feedback. Â You can work on an existing hash tag or have us start a new one. tweet at us, to get involved, or start using the hash tag that is relevant to your tweets and we will look into adding you to our curated list.

Who is Bmore Local?

Alan and Heather started this up, but this is a group effort. We have both Curators, people who have offered to take a lead in finding event mavens and getting them involved, and the event mavens themselves.
